About FRED

FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) is a database maintained by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis with over 800,000 economic time series from dozens of national and international sources. Data spans annual, quarterly, monthly, weekly, and daily frequencies.

Commonly Used Series

CPIAUCSL — Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers PCE — Personal Consumption Expenditures EXPINF1YR — Expected Inflation Rate over 1 Year EXPINF30YR — Expected Inflation Rate over 30 Years UNRATE — Unemployment Rate PAYEMS — All Employees, Total Nonfarm GDPC1 — Real Gross Domestic Product INDPRO — Industrial Production Index FEDFUNDS — Federal Funds Effective Rate DGS10 — 10-Year Treasury Constant Maturity Rate DGS30 — 30-Year Treasury Constant Maturity Rate TB3MS — 3-Month Treasury Bill: Secondary Market Rate MORTGAGE30US — 30-Year Fixed Rate Mortgage Average T10Y2Y — 10-Year Minus 2-Year Treasury Spread M2SL — M2 Money Stock VIXCLS — CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) UMCSENT — University of Michigan: Consumer Sentiment DJIA — Dow Jones Industrial Average SP500 — S&P 500 NASDAQCOM — NASDAQ Composite Index DHHNGSP — Henry Hub Natural Gas Spot Price WTISPLC — WTI Crude Oil Spot Price
